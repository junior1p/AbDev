05 — How It Works

The Developability Pipeline

AbDev combines multi-task deep learning with large-scale therapeutic antibody benchmarking to provide actionable developability predictions.

1
Step 01
Sequence Input & Preprocessing
Accepts VH, VL, or single-chain scFv formats. Sequences are validated, padded/truncated to 650 AA max, and tokenized using the 20-amino-acid vocabulary plus special tokens.
2
Step 02
Multi-Task Transformer Encoding
A 12-layer transformer encoder processes the sequence through shared ESM-2-style attention, producing per-residue embeddings used across all prediction heads.
3
Step 03
TAP Score Regression
Four regression heads predict thermostability, solubility, expressibility, and low immunogenicity scores (0–100). Scores are aggregated into a weighted composite TAP score.
4
Step 04
Liability Classification
A multi-label classifier scans for 7 developability liability types: aggregation hotspots, glycosylation sites, deamidation motifs, oxidation sites, isomerization regions, protease cleavage sites, and charged patches.
5
Step 05
Benchmark Comparison
Final scores are compared against a database of 1,200+ therapeutic antibodies (approved + clinical-stage) to rank the candidate and provide context-specific recommendations.
🔬

Physics-Informed Features

Sequence embeddings augmented with computed properties: hydrophobicity, charge, isoelectric point, and CDR canonical structures.

📊

Therapeutic Benchmark

Trained and evaluated against a curated set of 1,200+ approved and clinical-stage therapeutic antibodies with known developability profiles.

⚡

Fast Inference

Optimized single-sequence inference completes in under 30 seconds. Batch processing available for up to 100 sequences per request.

06 — Metrics Reference

Understanding TAP Scores

TAP (Therapeutic Antibody Predictability) scores are composite developability metrics ranging from 0–100. Higher scores indicate better predicted developability.

Score Range Rating Interpretation Typical Action
80–100 Excellent Highly developable; similar to approved therapeutics Proceed to CMC with confidence
60–79 Good Developable with standard developability panel Continue optimization; minor risks addressable
40–59 Moderate Moderate developability; specific liabilities identified Engineering required; re-evaluate after mutation
<40 High Risk Significant developability concerns; likely to fail Re-design or select alternative clone

Liability Types Detected

  • → Aggregation hotspots (hydrophobic patches)
  • → N-linked glycosylation sites ( Asn-X-Ser/Thr )
  • → Deamidation motifs ( Asn-Gly, Asn-Ser )
  • → Oxidation-sensitive residues ( Met, Trp, Cys )
  • → Isomerization sites ( Asp-Gly sequences )
  • → Protease cleavage motifs ( dibasic, cathepsin )
  • → Extreme charged patches (|ΔpI| > 2.0)

Benchmark Datasets

  • → 430 FDA-approved therapeutic antibodies
  • → 780 clinical-stage candidates (Phase I–III)
  • → 12,000+ recombinant mAb expressions
  • → 3,500 aggregation assay measurements
  • → 1,200 viscosity measurements at high concentration
